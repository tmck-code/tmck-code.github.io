Why it beats Option A

Offloading the SPA to S3 and both APIs to Lambda shrinks the box that must run 24/7 from an 8 GB instance to a 2 GB one. Everything you moved is near-free — so the whole saving is that one smaller always-on instance. That's why the floor can't drop much further: a database has to stay up.

The price is engineering, not dollars
FastAPI → Lambda (Mangum adapter), a build/deploy pipeline for the S3 SPA, and an arm64 image rebuild (stack is amd64-only today). Cheapest to run, most expensive to build — the mirror image of Option B.
You own the database
No RDS → no managed patching, no PITR. Backups are DIY: pg_dump → S3 on a schedule, or EBS snapshots. Acceptable for a low-stakes DB; a real risk if data loss hurts.
Watch the 2 GB box under Chrome
Headless Chrome wants 2 GB shm; alongside Postgres on a 2 GB instance it's tight during the twice-daily scrape. Add swap, or step up to t4g.medium (+~$16) for safety.
Cold starts are fine here
Low, bursty traffic means occasional Lambda cold starts on the APIs — a sub-second first hit, invisible for this workload. No provisioned concurrency needed.

Hetzner — architecture & cost

The cheapest option anywhere (~$11–13/mo) — but no Australian region. Germany / Finland / US only means +~250 ms to AU users and tender data leaves Australia. Everything self-hosted on one VM; there is no managed Postgres to buy.

The compute is unbeatable; the price is paid in data residency and latency, plus owning DB backups and patching yourself.

No AU region
+250 ms to AU users and tender data sits offshore — likely a dealbreaker for AU gov data. This is the real cost, not the dollars.
No managed Postgres
DIY backups and patching — pg_dump to object storage on a schedule, and you own every version upgrade. Fine for a low-stakes DB, a real risk if data loss hurts.
Otherwise unbeatable
Generous 20 TB traffic included, then €1/TB overage. Nothing else at this price runs the whole stack with headroom to spare.
