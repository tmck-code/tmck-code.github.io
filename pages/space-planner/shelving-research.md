# Shed Shelving Plan — Research Report

**Shed:** 2200 W × 1490 D × 1750 H (mm) · door centered on the 2200 mm wall
**Lawnmower:** 1200 L × 570 W × 1000 H (mm)
**Priority:** Heavy-duty / durable · mixed heights OK · half-height OK

> Method: fan-out web search across AU vendors → 20 sources fetched → 80 falsifiable claims extracted → 25 adversarially verified (24 confirmed, 1 killed).

---

## The finding that drives everything: height kills most heavy-duty units

Your shed ceiling is **1750 mm**. Almost every genuinely heavy-duty steel unit sold in Australia is built **1800–2090 mm tall** and *will not fit* — including your reference Pinnacle (2090 mm) and its 600 kg/shelf adjustable sibling (1830 mm, only 80 mm too tall).

The winning strategy is therefore either:

1. the few heavy units that ship **under 1700 mm**, or
2. exploiting your **"half-height is fine"** allowance to run a bombproof low bench.

**Geometry is generous.** A back-wall shelf (400 mm deep) + lawnmower parked sideways in front (570 mm deep) = 970 mm, leaving a ~520 mm walkway inside your 1490 mm depth. Everything below fits with margin.

## Recommendation

**Option A** is the sweet spot: genuinely heavy-duty (200 kg/shelf), full-height-usable, full back-wall coverage, fits as-is with no cutting, ~$680. If you truly want industrial 1000 kg capacity and accept a low back bench, **Option C** delivers it via the half-height trick you already approved.

## Before you buy — verify 3 things

1. **Confirm the Pinnacle 900-wide and Stormor prices** on their AU pages today (NZ-mirror data; not shown on the AU listing).
2. **Dimension order varies** by retailer (some H×W×D, some W×D×H). Every "fits / doesn't fit" verdict above was height-cross-checked, but re-read the spec tab.
3. **Rack It half-height build** — confirm in-store that an 1800 mm beam + 916 mm uprights + the 1000 kg shelf rating combine in one bay (uprights sold separately from the bundle).
