# Bingo in Victoria — Legal/Regulatory Parameters (VGCCC)

**Scope:** Bingo conducted by/for declared community or charitable organisations under **Chapter 8 of the Gambling Regulation Act 2003** (the "Act"), the **Gambling Regulations 2015** (the "Regulations", authorised Version 011, in force 3 June 2025), and the **Rules of Bingo** (Victoria Government Gazette S 462, 11 Nov 2010, made under s 8.4.2D of the Act). Regulated by the VGCCC.

A note on legal hierarchy used below: the **Act** sets the framework and offences; the **Regulations** set the operating conditions/percentages; the **Rules of Bingo** govern game conduct (drawing, tickets, jackpot announcements). Wherever the VGCCC website paraphrases, the figure has been traced back to its statutory source and quoted.

---

## 1. Prize limits, jackpots and rollover

There is **no maximum cap on total prize value** per game or session, and **no statutory cap on individual prizes or jackpots** in the Act, Regulations, or Rules. The controls are *minimum* prize floors plus a large-prize notification trigger.

**Minimum prizes (Regulation 73, "Prizes"):**

> "(1) … the amount of prizes distributed—
> (a) in a bingo game must be **not less than 20% of the gross receipts for the game**; and
> (b) in any period of 7 consecutive days must be **not less than 50% and not more than 90%** of the total of the gross receipts for all bingo games in all sessions conducted in that period."
> — https://content.legislation.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2025-06/15-55sra011-authorised.pdf (Reg 73(1))

So per game the prize floor is 20% of that game's gross receipts; across any rolling 7-day window prizes must total **50%–90%** of gross receipts. (Note: this is a floor/ceiling on prizes, not a cap on any single prize.)

**Prizes must be paid at game end:** Reg 73(3) — "prize money won in a bingo game must be paid at the end of the game"; mirrored in Rule 4.7 of the Rules of Bingo (gazette PDF).

**Jackpots / rollover / snowball (Rules of Bingo, Rule 7; Reg 73(2)):**

- A jackpot pool may carry forward "in a later game in that session" or "in a game in a later session in a rolling jackpot sequence" (Rule 7.1, gazette S 462).
- Before the first game where a jackpot operates, the Caller must announce how/when it is distributed and, for a rolling jackpot, the carried-forward balance (Rule 7.2). After the last game the closing balance and next session details must be announced (Rule 7.3).
- Reg 73(2): "if more than one jackpot pool is maintained concurrently, **only one of the jackpot pools may be in operation in a session**." — same authorised PDF.
- No dollar cap on jackpot size is prescribed. (The VRGF research report notes the largest jackpot it heard of was $450,000 and recommended caps — but no cap is currently in the law. Source: https://responsiblegambling.vic.gov.au/documents/975/VRGF_RR_LFS-BingoVIC_publish.pdf, p.viii.)

**Large-prize notification (≥ $20,000):** If a session's prize value will/expected to exceed **$20,000**, the organisation must notify the VGCCC at least **3 business days** beforehand (notification-of-bingo-prizes-in-excess-of-$20,000 form). — https://www.vgccc.vic.gov.au/for-gambling-providers/community-and-charitable-gaming/bingo and .../bingo/bingo-forms-and-rules. This is a *notification trigger only*, not a cap. (Do not confuse with the separate $20,000 **raffle** permit-exemption threshold in s 8.2.3 of the Act.)

**Prize payment method (Act s 8.4.7A):** A prize of **$1,000 or more** must not be paid in cash — only by cheque (not payable to cash) or EFT; and for EFT of at least $1,000 the funds "are not transferred until at least **24 hours** after the request." Penalty: 60 penalty units. — Gambling Regulation Act 2003 s 8.4.7A.

## 2. Financial / community-benefit rules

**There is NO fixed statutory percentage of net proceeds that must be paid to the declared community/charitable purpose for bingo.** Unlike some gaming, the Act/Regulations do not prescribe an "X% to charity" formula for bingo. The community benefit is protected indirectly by (a) the prize floors (Reg 73), (b) a cap on operator expenses (Reg 77), and (c) a cap on the operator fee (Act s 8.4.6). This was confirmed by reviewing the full bingo provisions of both the Act and the Regulations; no community-benefit percentage is specified.

**Operator fee — max 2% of gross receipts (Act s 8.4.6(2)(e)):**

> "the maximum fee, determined in accordance with the regulations and **not exceeding 2% of the gross receipts**, to be paid to the bingo centre operator for each session of bingo."
> — Gambling Regulation Act 2003 s 8.4.6(2)(e). Plus s 8.4.6(2A): if the CCO provides any assistance in conducting the session/operating the centre, **no fee** is payable.

**Operator expenses cap — 60% of gross proceeds (Act s 8.4.7 + Regulation 77):**

- Act s 8.4.7: a bingo centre operator "must not require a community or charitable organisation to pay … any expenses exceeding the maximum amount (if any) prescribed by the regulations." Penalty: 60 penalty units.
- Regulation 77 (prescribed maximum), verbatim:

> "For the purposes of section 8.4.7 of the Act, the prescribed maximum amount in relation to each session of bingo is—
> (a) if the session is in a **rolling jackpot sequence—60% of the amount remaining after deducting from the gross proceeds for that session the amount paid into the jackpot pool** for distribution as prize money in a later session in a rolling jackpot sequence; or
> (b) in all other cases—**60% of the gross proceeds of the session**."
> — https://content.legislation.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2025-06/15-55sra011-authorised.pdf (Reg 77).

Important: that single 60% figure is the *entire* prescribed maximum for operator expenses; there is no separate per-line expense breakdown in Reg 77. Because **"gross proceeds = gross receipts − prizes paid"** (Act s 8.1.2, see §5), the 60% cap applies to receipts *after* prizes. So the practical maximum an operator can extract from a non-jackpot session is the ≤2% fee (of gross receipts) plus up to 60% of (gross receipts − prizes). What's left is the CCO's net proceeds. There is no enumerated "allowable expenses" list in the bingo Regulations — the cap is a single dollar ceiling, not an itemised list.

**What counts in the return / "total expenses":** Reg 69(1)(b) requires reporting "the total expenses (including catering and the fees paid to the holder of a bingo centre operator's licence)" — so catering and operator fees are treated as expenses for reporting.

## 3. Ticket / book / flyer / session-structure rules

**No price cap on bingo tickets/books/flyers** is prescribed in the Act, Regulations, or Rules. The only price rule is consistency within a game:

> Rule 5.4: "The price of Bingo tickets must be the same for each ticket in a game but may vary from game to game." — gazette S 462.

- **No admission charge:** Rule 5.2 — "No charge may be made for admission to premises for the purpose of playing Bingo."
- **No statutory limit on number of books/flyers** sold per session. For *electronic* tickets only, Rule 5.7 sets the per-player max as "determined by the Supervisor and displayed on a notice at the point of sale." ("Flyers" — single-game sheets sold on top of books — are an industry term, not separately regulated; see VRGF glossary.)
- **Session structure cap:** a session = no more than **30 games within an 8-hour period** (Act s 8.1.2 definition — see §5). The number of games per session and frequency are set by the organisation and notified to the VGCCC (Act s 8.4.2A(2)).
- **Ticket standards** (Rule 8, gazette): tickets must follow the grid for the Type (e.g. Type 1: 3×5 grid, 15 numbers 1–90); books printed in consecutive games must be consecutively numbered and "each game in the series must be printed on paper of a different colour from the four preceding games."
- **No online bingo:** Act s 8.4.7B prohibits conducting a session or selling tickets via a website/online platform (except on-premises electronic ticket devices). Penalty: 60 penalty units each.

## 4. Who can run it / licensing

**Only a declared community or charitable organisation can run bingo for profit, itself or via a licensed bingo centre operator (Act s 8.2.4):**

> "(1) A session of bingo may be conducted … by— (a) a community or charitable organisation; or (b) a bingo centre operator on behalf of a community or charitable organisation."
> — Gambling Regulation Act 2003 s 8.2.4. Confirmed on https://www.vgccc.vic.gov.au/for-gambling-providers/community-and-charitable-gaming/bingo.

**"Free/entertainment" exemption (s 8.2.4(2) and s 8.2.4A):** No declaration is needed if **either** no fee is charged to participate, **or** the whole of the gross receipts is distributed as prizes during the session. Non-CCO persons may also run bingo under s 8.2.4A only if it is not advertised/open to the public and not for commercial benefit (e.g. nursing-home/social bingo).

**Becoming a declared organisation (Act ss 8.3.1–8.3.3):** Apply to the VGCCC (form + prescribed fee). The Commission may declare an organisation if satisfied it is conducted in good faith for a philanthropic/benevolent purpose (art, culture, science, religion, education, charity), a prescribed sporting/recreational club, or a political party (s 8.3.3(1)). There is **no minimum dollar threshold** for declaration — it is a qualitative test, not a turnover trigger.

**Notification to conduct bingo (Act s 8.4.2A):** A declared organisation must notify the VGCCC at least **7 days** before the first session, giving premises, days/dates, start/finish times, frequency, number of games per session, the ADI account, and a nominated responsible person aged 18+. Changes must be notified within 7 days.

**Bingo centre operator licence (Act Part 5, ss 8.5.1–8.5.6):**

- s 8.5.1: "A person must not have the management or control of a bingo centre unless the person holds a bingo centre operator's licence." Penalty: **100 penalty units**.
- s 8.5.2(2): cannot apply if the person is a venue operator (poker-machine), an associate of one, or a casino operator.
- Grant criteria (s 8.5.6): applicant and associates "suitable," premises suitable, Responsible Gambling Code of Conduct.
- Application requires (per VGCCC): accountant/auditor statement, financial-institution letter, credit report, creditors listing, National Police Certificate (≤3 months old for sole traders/partnerships), 3-year business plan/financial projections, proof of tenure, floor plans. — https://www.vgccc.vic.gov.au/for-gambling-providers/community-and-charitable-gaming/bingo/bingo-centre-operators.

**Commercial raffle organiser:** A separate licence category exists (Act Part 5A, ss 8.5A.x) for commercial *raffle* organisers; it is not a bingo authority.

## 5. Definitions (verbatim, Act s 8.1.2 unless noted)

- **Session of bingo:** "a session comprising **no more than 30 games of bingo conducted within an 8 hour period**." (Act s 8.1.2)
- **Gross receipts:** "in relation to a bingo game or a session of bingo games, means **the total amount paid by players in that game or session for tickets**." (Act s 8.1.2)
- **Gross proceeds:** "in relation to a bingo game or session of bingo games, means **the gross receipts less the prizes paid** for that game or session." (Act s 8.1.2)
- **Net proceeds:** not separately defined for bingo in s 8.1.2; the **return** (Reg 69(1)(e)) requires reporting "the net proceeds of bingo" as a distinct line alongside gross receipts, expenses, and prizes — i.e. what remains for the organisation after prizes and allowable expenses/fees.
- **Game:** not statutorily defined; the Rules treat a game as one round won by the first player(s) to complete the predetermined pattern/number set and call (Rule 4.1). VRGF glossary: "a game of bingo runs for the time it takes for a person to correctly call bingo."
- **Book / flyer:** not statutorily defined. Industry/VRGF usage: a *book* is a set of consecutive-game tickets; a *flyer* is a single-game sheet sold in addition to books. The Rules regulate ticket *books* (Rule 8: consecutive numbering, different paper colour from prior 4 games).
- **Rolling jackpot sequence (Regulations, defined term):** "the sessions of bingo over which a rolling jackpot operates."
- **Type of Bingo (Rule 2):** Type 1 = numbers 1–90; Type 2 = 1–80; Type 3 = 1–75. Only one Type per game (Rule 2.2). **Version** = the pattern/amount of numbers to win (Rule 3), must be notified to the VGCCC before use.

## 6. Other notable parameters

- **Audit threshold — $50,000 (Regulation 69(4)):** A return must include an auditor's certificate **unless** "the gross receipts for the year of the return **do not exceed $50,000**" and the running sheets/bank records have been reviewed by the responsible person and an office-holder.
- **Annual return — by 1 October (Reg 69(1)):** A CCO must, "before **1 October** in each year that bingo is conducted," lodge a return for the year ending the preceding 30 June, showing per month: gross receipts; total expenses (incl. catering and operator fees); serial numbers of unsold tickets; prizes paid; net proceeds; and the balance of each jackpot pool. Signed by the responsible person and an office-holder. (Bingo centre operators lodge by **30 September** per VGCCC.)
- **Banking (Reg 71):** Gross proceeds (less any jackpot pool retained) must be banked into the VGCCC-approved account within **3 days** (bingo centre) or **5 days** (other premises) of the session.
- **Single approved account (Act s 8.6.1):** a single ADI account in Victoria for all Chapter 8 transactions. Penalty 100 penalty units.
- **Unsold tickets retained 6 months (Reg 72)**; **running sheets** completed per game (Reg 70(2)(b), Reg 79). VGCCC states running sheets kept 3 years.
- **Age 18+:** Players, responsible persons and authorised supervisors must be 18+ (Rule 5.1; Reg 70(2)(a)).
- **GST:** Not addressed in the VGCCC bingo material or these gambling instruments — GST is a Commonwealth matter (ATO), not specified in Victorian bingo regulation.
- **Penalty unit value (context):** $203.51 from 1 July 2025–30 June 2026 (was $197.59 in 2024-25). So 60 penalty units ≈ $12,210; 100 ≈ $20,351. — https://www.legalaid.vic.gov.au/penalty-units.

## Key caveats

1. **There is no fixed "% to community purpose" for bingo** — the model is prize floors + operator-take caps, not a charity-percentage formula.
2. The prize percentages are in **Reg 73**, not Reg 70 as some secondary summaries state.
3. The $20,000 figure is a *notification* trigger for bingo (and separately a *raffle* permit-exemption threshold), not a prize cap.
4. GST is not covered by these Victorian instruments.
