How it works. Cards are real Australian 90-ball housie tickets (15 numbers, 5 per row, valid columns; a book of 6 covers all 90). Each game draws balls until the first full house — play stops there, exactly as in the hall. Roulette games pay the value of the winning number (1–50 → $50, above → face value; ×3 for triple). Rolling-flyer games pay the jackpot only if the full house lands inside the call limit, otherwise a smaller in-house prize. The program, prices and prizes come from each venue's weekly flyers; the legal session cap is 30 games / 8 hours (Gambling Regulation Act 2003). A simulation for curiosity — not a prediction, and not advice.
